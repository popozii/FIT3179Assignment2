Cultural Heritage & Global Tourism

Exploring the Relationship Between UNESCO World Heritage Sites and International Tourism

About This Visualization

This interactive dashboard explores the intersection of cultural heritage preservation and global tourism patterns. UNESCO World Heritage Sites represent humanity's most treasured cultural and natural landmarks, while international tourism data reveals global travel trends and economic impacts.

The visualization combines data from UNESCO World Heritage Centre and World Bank tourism statistics to provide insights into how cultural heritage sites are distributed globally and how they relate to tourism patterns across different regions and time periods.

Data Sources: UNESCO World Heritage Centre (2025) & World Bank - International Tourism Arrivals

UNESCO World Heritage

Global Distribution of UNESCO World Heritage Sites

This interactive map shows all UNESCO World Heritage Sites worldwide. Cultural sites are shown in blue, natural sites in green, and mixed sites in orange. Click on the legend to filter by site type, and hover over any point to see detailed information about each heritage site.

Top 15 Countries by Number of Heritage Sites

This chart displays the countries with the most UNESCO World Heritage Sites. Italy and China lead with the highest numbers, reflecting their rich cultural histories. The red dashed line shows the average number of sites among these top countries.

Global Tourism Patterns

Global Tourist Arrivals Over Time

This timeline shows the total number of international tourist arrivals worldwide from 1995 to 2020. Use the brush selection tool to focus on specific time periods. Note the significant impact of global events like the 2020 pandemic on tourism numbers.

Heritage & Tourism Analysis

Heritage Sites Growth Over Time

This area chart shows the cumulative growth of UNESCO World Heritage Sites since the program's inception. Compare this growth pattern with the tourism timeline above to observe potential correlations between heritage designation and tourism interest.
